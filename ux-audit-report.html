Hubble Mobile App — UX Bug Report

A deliberately critical review of the Expo / React Native inspector app. Every finding below was verified twice against the actual source; unconfirmed and rejected claims were dropped.

110 confirmed issues 13 high · 55 medium · 42 low 14 review lanes · 312 component files Generated 2026-07-01
D+The happy path works and ships. Everything around it — error states, cancel/back, offline, accessibility, and localization edges — is unfinished, and several gaps are dangerous in a product that generates legally-binding safety records.

Executive summary

This is a capable app on its happy path: an inspector who is online, taps deliberately, never hits an error, and never changes their mind can complete an inspection and generate a PDF. The audit was not interested in that path — it stress-tested the edges, and the edges are where a regulated field tool actually lives.

The recurring failure is that the app cannot tell the difference between “failed”, “empty”, and “done.” A network error becomes “you have no projects.” A locked breathalyzer log looks re-openable. A cosmetic checklist gate sits next to a button that submits anyway. A dead “Generate PDF” button looks identical to a working one. In a compliance tool, a confident-but-wrong screen is worse than an honest error — an inspector can sign off on a false state.

The second theme is silent loss: back-buttons and cancels across at least eleven editing surfaces discard work with no “Discard changes?” prompt (an exit-confirmation component exists in the code but is never rendered), destructive actions like certificate-delete and log-close fire on one unconfirmed tap, and a second tap on submit/share can duplicate a record or double-spend the user’s PDF quota.

Finally, two whole audiences are underserved: screen-reader users (only 15 of 312 files carry any accessibility label) and, ironically, Georgian users — the scaffold safety guide is English-only, an equipment form shows corrupted transliterated labels, and a sub-page is mis-titled “Orders.”

The nine patterns behind the bugs

01

Errors are indistinguishable from “empty” and “success”

9 findings · 1 high

The single most damaging pattern in the app. Multiple list and detail screens drop their error state entirely: a failed network fetch renders the exact same “no projects”, “nothing here yet”, or infinite skeleton that a genuinely-empty account would. An offline inspector is told their projects, incidents, or briefings simply don’t exist — and is even prompted to re-create them.

02

Back / Cancel silently destroys work

11 findings · 2 high

Almost no editing surface guards unsaved changes. Wizards, the signer form, the risk-assessment editor, the report-slide editor, profile, the create/edit project sheet, the signature pad and the photo annotator all discard in-progress input on a stray back-tap or backdrop-tap, with no “Discard changes?” prompt. An `ExitConfirmationModal` component even exists in the codebase — it is simply never rendered.

03

Destructive & irreversible actions fire on a single unconfirmed tap

4 findings · 2 high

For a product that produces legally-meaningful safety documents, the guardrails on dangerous actions are thin. Deleting a certificate, closing a breathalyzer log (which then locks it forever), and other destructive actions execute immediately with no confirmation — and confirmation is applied inconsistently across otherwise-identical flows.

04

A second tap means a duplicate record — or a double charge

5 findings · 1 high

Primary submit / next / share buttons lack double-tap and in-flight guards. Rapid or anxious taps (normal on a slow field connection) create duplicate breathalyzer logs, duplicate signer records, re-trigger OTP, or — on the incident success screen — can bill the user’s limited PDF quota twice.

05

Accessibility is effectively absent

12 findings · 2 high

Only 15 of 312 component files set an accessibilityLabel and only 12 set an accessibilityRole. The base Button primitive exposes no role, the theme selector never announces its selected state, icon-only controls (password eye, keypad backspace, map marker, clear-signature) are unlabeled, several tap targets fall below the 44pt minimum, and status is frequently conveyed by colour alone. A screen-reader user cannot reliably operate this app.

06

Georgian users still hit English and broken text

6 findings · 2 high

The UI is Georgian, the users are Georgian safety inspectors — yet the 3D scaffold guide renders its entire step-by-step safety guidance in English only, an equipment inspection shows Latin-transliterated (corrupted) Georgian labels, a project sub-page is mis-titled “Orders”, long Georgian errors truncate in the toast, and the PDF-language preference is wired to nothing.

07

Controls that lie: buttons that look functional but do nothing

5 findings · 1 high

Several visible, tappable controls are cosmetic or dead: a “Generate PDF” button in the report editor produces nothing, a checklist completion gate is purely cosmetic while the button stays enabled, a share action silently no-ops when data is missing, a draft-order row is inert, and a finish-navigation timeout is dead code.

08

Dead ends: screens you can enter but not leave

6 findings

The expired password-reset screen, a failed inspection-create, a completed order with no PDF, an unreachable remote-signer add flow, a harness close-button that doesn’t close, and a “View PDF” that just goes back — all strand the user with no forward or backward affordance.

09

Silent failures: it failed, but the app said nothing

7 findings

Photo uploads, risk-assessment uploads, external regulation links, breathalyzer serial saves, geocoding and phone-dial links can all fail with zero user-facing feedback — the user believes the action succeeded when it did not.
